Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most often from applicants and trainees.

Do I need a university degree to apply?

No — we do not require a degree for any of our roles. We care about your skills, attitude, and potential. Many of our current team members have BTEC Level 3 qualifications or are self-taught. A portfolio of personal projects is worth far more to us than any specific certificate.

How do I know if I am ready to apply?

If you can build a basic webpage with HTML and CSS, and you understand what JavaScript does, you are likely ready for the Trainee role. For Junior roles, you should have built several projects and feel comfortable with JavaScript. We recommend checking out our Courses page if you want to build up your skills first.

Can I apply for more than one role at a time?

Yes, you can apply for up to two roles simultaneously. Just mention both roles in your application and we will consider you for each one separately. We will always interview you for the role we think suits you best.

How long does the application process take?

Typically two to three weeks from submitting your application to receiving a job offer. We aim to respond to all applications within five working days. If you have not heard from us after seven working days, feel free to follow up at jobs@enfielddev.co.uk.

What is the technical interview like?

It is a relaxed, one-hour session with two of our developers. There are no trick questions or whiteboard algorithms. You will walk through one or two of your own projects and then complete a short practical task that takes around 20 minutes. We want to see how you think and communicate, not test your memory under pressure.

Is the role fully remote, hybrid, or fully in the office?

Trainee roles start with three months in the office full-time so we can give you proper face-to-face support. After that, they move to a hybrid model (typically 3 days in office, 2 at home). Junior roles offer hybrid working from day one.

What support do new starters receive?

Every new starter is paired with a dedicated mentor for their first six months. You will have a weekly one-to-one with your mentor to review progress and set goals. There are also team code reviews, a Friday learning session each week, and an open-door policy with all senior staff.

What if I struggle during my first weeks?

That is completely normal and expected. Almost everyone feels overwhelmed at first. Your mentor is there specifically to support you through those early weeks. We would rather you ask for help early than struggle alone. If you are having a hard time, you can also speak to Aisha in our People team confidentially.

Are there training opportunities once I join?

Yes. Every team member has an annual training budget (£500 for Trainees, up to £1,000 for Juniors) which can be spent on courses, books, conferences, or certifications. We also have a dedicated learning session every Friday afternoon.

What accessibility support is available during the interview process?

We are committed to making our recruitment process accessible to everyone. If you require any adjustments — such as extra time, a different format, or an accessible venue — please let us know when you apply or contact Aisha at jobs@enfielddev.co.uk and we will do our best to accommodate you.

Do you offer work experience or shadowing opportunities?

We occasionally offer one-week work experience placements for students in Year 10–13. These are limited and competitive. If you are interested, send a short email to info@enfielddev.co.uk with your school, year group, and why you are interested in web development.

Wellbeing & Mental Health

Starting a new career can be stressful. Here are some resources that may help.

Mind — Mental Health Charity

mind.org.uk

Mind provides advice and support to empower anyone experiencing a mental health problem. Their website has guides on anxiety, stress, and adjusting to new work situations.

Visit Mind

Young Minds

youngminds.org.uk

The UK's leading charity for young people's mental health. Useful resources for those starting their first job or managing anxiety around applications and interviews.

Visit Young Minds

NHS — Mental Health Support

nhs.uk/mental-health

The NHS provides free mental health support including talking therapies (IAPT) which you can refer yourself to without needing to see a GP first.

Visit NHS
